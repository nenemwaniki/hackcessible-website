A hackathon for problems that don't fit a brief.

Hackcessible pairs AKU medical students with KU biomedical engineers and assigns each team a real challenger — a person living with a condition that off-the-shelf assistive tech doesn't quite serve. Modelled on Sheffield's University of Sanctuary work.

The brief comes after the visit.

Teams meet their challenger first. They sit in living rooms. They eat lunch with parents. The brief is whatever surfaces from those visits, not a product spec written in a faculty office.

By the end of the cohort, every team has shipped something a family can keep using — and a written record of what they got wrong, so the next cohort starts ten steps ahead.

Four things every team should hear first.

Cross-cohort lessons written for the team that walks in next year. They are meant to save them a week each.

01

Assess before you assign.

The team that meets the challenger first, and writes the brief second, ships something that fits.

02

Caregivers are co-designers.

Not interview subjects. The person who knows the daily rhythm is on the design team.

03

Put a clinician in the design room.

Not on call. In the room while sketches are happening.

04

Real-world beats clinical elegance.

The smartwatch was elegant. It stayed in the drawer. Optimise for what is actually used.
